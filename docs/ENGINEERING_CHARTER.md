# Engineering Charter

## Purpose

This repository documents my journey toward becoming an expert DevOps and Platform Engineer. It serves as a structured engineering workspace containing learning material, projects, architectural decisions, reference documentation, notes, and practical implementations.

The objective is not only to learn tools, but to understand the engineering principles, trade-offs, and decision-making processes behind them.

---

## Vision

Develop the ability to:

- Design reliable and scalable systems.
- Build and automate infrastructure.
- Make cost-conscious engineering decisions.
- Understand modern DevOps practices from first principles.
- Build strong first-principles understanding that remains valuable for years.
- Leverage AI effectively without becoming dependent on it.
- Communicate engineering decisions clearly.
- Continuously improve through structured learning.

---

## Learning Principles

This journey follows these principles:

1. Understand the problem before the tool.
2. Learn concepts before commands.
3. Prefer understanding over memorization.
4. Build to reinforce understanding.
5. Use analogies and mental models to simplify complex concepts.
6. Document knowledge as a long-term engineering handbook.
7. Continuously question assumptions and engineering decisions.
8. Learn how modern AI systems apply engineering concepts.
9. Focus on engineering thinking rather than tool usage.

---

## Documentation Philosophy

Documentation is treated as part of the engineering process rather than an afterthought.

The repository is intended to evolve into a personal engineering handbook rather than a collection of session notes.

Reference documents are designed for quick lookup and long-term revision.

Each document should remain:

- Clear
- Concise
- Consistent
- Maintainable

Documentation should evolve as understanding grows while preserving previously established knowledge.

---

## Engineering Standards

When learning a new topic, the objective is to achieve four levels of mastery:

- **Understand** — Explain the concept using first principles.
- **Build** — Implement the concept independently.
- **Troubleshoot** — Diagnose and resolve common failures.
- **Teach** — Explain the concept clearly using simple language and real-world analogies.

---

## AI Usage Philosophy

AI is treated as an engineering assistant rather than a replacement for understanding.

AI should be used to:

- Accelerate learning.
- Explore engineering alternatives.
- Review designs and implementations.
- Challenge assumptions.
- Improve productivity.
- Understand how modern AI systems apply engineering concepts.

Engineering decisions should always be made through reasoning and understanding rather than blind acceptance of AI-generated answers.

---

## Success Metrics

Progress will be measured by the ability to:

- Build complete working solutions.
- Explain architectural decisions clearly.
- Make informed engineering trade-offs.
- Solve unfamiliar problems independently.
- Retain engineering understanding that remains valuable over time.
- Maintain a professional engineering portfolio.
- Demonstrate consistent engineering growth.

---

## Living Document

This charter is a living document.

It may evolve as experience grows, but every change should preserve the core philosophy of understanding first, building with purpose, and continuously improving as an engineer.
